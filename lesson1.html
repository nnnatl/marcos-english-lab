Dialogue

Barista: Hi there! What can I get for you today?
Marco: Hi, could I please get a flat white?
Barista: Sure! What size would you like — small, regular, or large?
Marco: Regular, please.
Barista: No worries. And for you?
Natalie: I’ll have an iced latte with oat milk, thanks.
Barista: Great. That’ll be $9.50 altogether.
Marco: (hands card) Here you go.
Barista: Thanks — just tap your card when you’re ready.

Key Vocabulary & Aussie Tips

PhraseMeaningAussie Tip
Flat whiteEspresso with steamed milk, less frothy than a latteVery common Aussie order
No worriesIt’s okay / You’re welcome / SureFriendly, widely used
AltogetherTotal costCommon in shops
Tap your cardUse contactless paymentWorks with cards & phones
Could I please get…Polite ordering phraseMore natural than “I want…”

Role‑Play

Scenario A (Customer): Greet → Order with “Could I please get…” → Ask about size/milk → Pay → Thank the barista.

Scenario B (Barista): Greet → Clarify size/milk → Give total → Prompt to tap card → Close with “Thanks / Have a good one!”.
